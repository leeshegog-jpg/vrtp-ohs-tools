5–8 words. Describes the core occurrence. Plain language — no jargon.
Must link clearly to the hazard. Format: There is a risk that [occurrence] may occur, which could result in [consequence], due to [context]. Risk cause may be combined into this field.
Optional if cause is already embedded in the risk description. Format: The risk may be caused by [immediate causes], due to [underlying/systemic causes].
State the rating, reference the matrix definition, cite evidence. Assess WITHOUT any controls in place.
State the rating, reference frequency from the matrix. Assess as though NO controls are in place.
Required for Extreme/High only. Clearly link risk to controls. List what is implemented, how each reduces risk, and why further measures are not reasonably practicable. Never write 'the risk is acceptable'.
Format: [Control] is in place to [prevent/reduce] [risk/consequence] by [mechanism]. State the action first — a procedure alone is not a control.
Audits and inspections are assurance activities, not primary controls. If Yes, the description must include all 7 required elements.
⚠ Audit/Inspection as Control — 7 elements required in description: 1. What is being verified (specific control/condition)  ·  2. Method (audit, inspection, test, sampling, observation)  ·  3. Frequency (defined interval or trigger)  ·  4. Responsible role  ·  5. Acceptance criteria (what compliant looks like)  ·  6. Evidence/records (checklist, CMMS, report)  ·  7. Escalation trigger (what happens if non-compliant)
Required if critical. Must include all five in order: Responsibility; Frequency; Performance Criteria; Evidence; Escalation of Failure.
Required for critical controls. Six components in order: Performance Parameter (what is measured, quantified) → Method & Tool → Sample Point (where) → Acceptance Threshold (pass/fail, numerical) → Frequency (tied to operating conditions) → Record & Escalation (what to record, who to notify, at what threshold).
Format: What to do → what to use → where/when → threshold → record it → who to notify at what level.
How and why do these controls reduce risk from inherent to current? Reference hierarchy level — admin controls cannot claim the same reduction as engineering.
